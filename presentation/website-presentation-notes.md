# Powerhouse Church Website Presentation Notes

## Slide 1: Title
- Introduce the website as a full digital ministry platform, not just a marketing page.
- Emphasize the three layers: public website, members portal, and admin operations.

## Slide 2: What The Website Solves
- Explain that the platform serves visitors, members, and leaders in one connected system.
- Highlight that the structure mirrors a real ministry funnel: discover, connect, follow through.

## Slide 3: Brand And First Impression
- Point out the warmth of the homepage and how clearly it communicates the church identity.
- Mention that the homepage combines emotional tone with practical actions like planning a visit or watching live.

## Slide 4: Visitor Journey
- Explain that the new-here flow is designed to reduce friction for first-time guests.
- Call out the ministry value: ushers, pastors, and kids workers can prepare before the person arrives.

## Slide 5: Ongoing Engagement
- Show that the website supports ongoing participation through events, sermons, prayer, giving, and live content.
- Mention that events are not static posts because they include RSVP, capacity, waitlist, and calendar features.

## Slide 6: Members Portal
- Describe the portal as the digital extension of discipleship during the week.
- Note the mix of personal tools, community tools, and leadership visibility.

## Slide 7: Admin And Operations
- Frame this as the part that helps ministry teams work more efficiently.
- Mention reports, event reminders, exports, prayer workflows, visit-plan follow-up, and audit tracking.

## Slide 8: Technical Foundation
- Briefly explain that the site is built on a modern React and TypeScript stack with Prisma and PostgreSQL.
- Mention that the app already includes testing and abuse protection on public submissions.

## Slide 9: Closing
- End by saying the platform helps the church welcome people, disciple members, and manage ministry with less friction.
- Use the suggested final line if you want a strong closing statement.
